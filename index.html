Education Demographics in Australia

Understanding Australia's Educational Landscape and the Citizens Who Engage with It.

As of the 2021 Australian Census, over 5.5 million people held a bachelor's degree, a 31% increase since 2016, with the most common degrees in the fields of Business and Management. Just over 3.6 million students were attending primary or secondary school, with 1.2 million students enrolled in university and 600,000 participating in vocational training. Since the census, these numbers have shown consistent growth, highlighting the increasing value placed on education.

Understanding these figures offers a foundation to analyse trends and patterns across various sectors of education. By identifying fields with lower enrolments or emerging gaps in specific qualifications, stakeholders can make informed decisions to improve educational outcomes. This may involve targeting specific degrees or training programmes that are underrepresented, allocating more resources to these areas, or developing policies aimed at addressing workforce shortages.

Education by Characteristics

Examining the Educational Characteristics Across Demographic Groups.

On average, most qualifications are predominantly held by individuals born in Australia, covering all qualification levels listed except postgraduate degrees. In contrast, nearly 60% of postgraduate degrees are held by citizens born overseas. This could be due to the increasing number of international students pursuing advanced studies in Australia, as well as skilled individuals seeking further qualifications to enhance their career prospects.

Government schools outnumber both Catholic and other non-government schools, with more than double the number of secondary schools and over triple the number of primary schools compared to Catholic schools, which have the second-most in both categories. Additionally, the overall count of primary schools exceeds that of secondary schools across all categories, reflecting the government's strategy to establish more primary schools.

Education by Study Field

Insights into the Distribution of Study Fields Across Citizens' Demographics.

Certain fields of study display a balanced representation of males and females, while others exhibit notable gender disparities. For example, "Creative Arts" and "Management and Commerce" demonstrate a similar gender distribution, with Management and Commerce recognized as the most commonly held degree in Australia, followed closely by Engineering.

In contrast, females are predominantly represented in degrees such as Education, Society and Culture, and Health, while males are more prevalent in fields like Architecture, Building, Engineering, and Information Technology, highlighting the traditional roles associated with these disciplines.

Overall, Agriculture and Environmental Studies emerge as the least commonly held degrees, closely followed by Information Technology.
