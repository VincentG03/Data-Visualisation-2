Education Demographics in Australia

Understanding Australia's Educational Landscape and the Citizens Who Engage with It.

As of the 2021 Australian Census, over 5.5 million people held a bachelor's degree, a 31% increase since 2016, with the most common degrees in the fields of Business and Management. Just over 3.6 million students were attending primary or secondary school, with 1.2 million students enrolled in university and 600,000 participating in vocational training. Since the census, these numbers have shown consistent growth, highlighting the increasing value placed on education.

Understanding these figures offers a foundation to analyse trends and patterns across various sectors of education. By identifying fields with lower enrolments or emerging gaps in specific qualifications, stakeholders can make informed decisions to improve educational outcomes. This may involve targeting specific degrees or training programmes that are underrepresented, allocating more resources to these areas, or developing policies aimed at addressing workforce shortages.
